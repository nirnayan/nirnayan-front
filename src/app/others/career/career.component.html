Job Description

Our organization is looking for the role of Microbiologist with outstanding research and analytical skills. We appreciate you in advance for taking a look at the list of responsibilities, criteria, and qualifications required. If you don’t fill all of the qualifications, you may still be considered depending on your level of experience. Qualifications for Microbiologist • MD Microbiology with at least 3 to 5 years of experience (essential). • Experience in Microbiology (Bacteriology, Virology, and parasitology), Serology, Mycology, and ImmunoFluro Assay (IFA) (essential). N.B: If the candidate has no experience in any of the above-mentioned domains then the organization will arrange for that training and the candidate will have to sign the company's bond. • Manual dexterity and visual acuity sufficient to perform tests and release the patient report. • Active current state licensure as a registered Microbiologist. Responsibilities for Microbiologist • To direct the concerned department of the Laboratory. • To supervise the work of technologists in the respective department. • To have a comprehensive knowledge of Microbiology (Bacteriology, Virology, and parasitology), Serology, Mycology, and immuno-fluro assay (IFA). • Responsible for the preparation of assigned departmental reports with interpretative summaries. • If any further suggestion is required after any investigation, it should be mentioned in the patient report. • Maintain knowledge of various research methods and perform all manual operations on various supplies. • Develop and maintain validation in all protocols for various environmental controls. • Perform laboratory analysis on all materials after appropriate sterilization. • Perform tests on component samples, identify any contamination, conduct an analysis, and initiate corrective measures. • Study various cultures of microorganisms in isolation according to standard inhibition and ensure control over moisture and temperature. • Monitor all physiological and morphological characteristics and identify microorganisms. • Perform audits on various environmental programs and perform reviews. • Has to maintain all the records conducted in the laboratory properly for both the laboratory and the licensing authority. • Provide advice and interpretation of test results and the appropriateness for further investigation. • To review, and interpret cases by analyzing laboratory findings and case investigation reports. • Work alongside laboratory technologists or biomedical scientists while they complete laboratory tests. • Provide continual training of the respective departmental staff based upon identified learning needs understandably and also continuing education regarding the use and limitations of each diagnostic investigation. • Responsibility for evaluating, planning, and implementing a goal of that department. • Participate in the development and validation of new assays in the respective department. • Ensure that the highest standards of quality and customer service are maintained. • To achieve any kind of accreditation(national and international), the quality to be maintained must be reviewed periodically and all responsibilities must be taken during the assessment. • Develop and document various microbiology laboratory processes and prepare final reports. • Develop and prepare a proper protocol and SOP for all the work and the roster of technical staff of the department should be made every month. • Carry out research and keep up to date with new information relevant to your field and every year at least 3 research papers should be published in a reputed international journal with the name of our organization. • Arrange CMEs, workshops, and webinars by collaborating with eminent personalities to uplift the standard of the organization.
